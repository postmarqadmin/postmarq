Stamps.

Not a like. Not a metric. Not a growth tool. Not something an algorithm counts to decide who deserves to be seen.

A stamp means you stopped, decided this was worth something, and chose to send it. It's recognition. It means: I saw this. It meant something to me. I wanted you to know.

Maybe you've been to that place. Maybe you love that movie. Maybe you just felt it. A stamp is the smallest possible way to say we have something in common without having to say anything at all.

No follower counts attached to it. No reach boosted because something got a lot of stamps. The number doesn't matter — who sent it does.

Over time, the people stamping your posts are the ones worth paying attention to. Not because the platform told you so — because they kept showing up, on their own, for no other reason than they wanted to. That's how your quorum finds you. Not through an algorithm. Through recognition.

That's the whole point. ⬡
